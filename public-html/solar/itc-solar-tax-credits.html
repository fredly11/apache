I.T.C. - Solar investment tax credit

TALK TO A REAL PERSON LEARN MORE
The ITC has been an important part of developing solar energy in the United States. Since its extension in 2008, prices for solar systems and panels have fallen year-to-year, enabling more homeowners to take advantage of solar energy than ever before. It’s now more affordable than ever for middle-class families in the U.S. to transition to solar energy.

The Solar Investment Tax Credit (ITC) allows homeowners a 26 percent federal tax credit on solar systems installed on their homes. The credit is equal to 26 percent of the total which is invested in the system.

Tax credits are reduced dollar-for-dollar in your income tax. The credit is deducted from the total amount you would otherwise pay the government.It’s possible to enter a contract with a solar company, where they utilize the federal tax credit instead, and you “purchase” solar energy from them—still a generous cost reduction on energy bills.

The ITC will only be active for a short time longer. Homeowners would be wise to act on this incentive before it’s gone. The ITC will stay at 26% through 2020 and will decrease again and end in 2021.
